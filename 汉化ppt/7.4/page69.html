7.4 Debugging
Reflect: How to ensure It’ll Never Happen Again
▪ Requirements:
– Were the requirements complete and correct? Perhaps they were
ambiguous, interpreted incorrectly, or misunderstood?
▪ Architecture or design:
– Was there an oversight within the architecture or design—something we
failed to take into account or allow for? Or perhaps they’re fine, but we
failed to follow the design correctly?
▪ Testing:
– Did we have adequate tests covering this area? Or maybe the error was in
the tests themselves?
▪ Construction:
– Perhaps the author made a simple mistake when writing the code, or
maybe they misunderstood some aspect of the underlying technology
(libraries, compilers, and so forth).
